# TIDEMARK

*A novel — built one chapter at a time.*

---

## What it's about

A hundred years or so from now, the sea took the old cities. What's left of Metro Manila lives stacked up in the **Spires** — huge towers sticking out of the water where Makati used to be. Everything you need to stay alive — water, power, work, how high up you're allowed to live — comes from **BANTAY**, the system that watches everyone and gives everyone a score. That score is your **tidemark**. It decides how much water you get, how high you live, and whether you're even worth the power it takes to keep you breathing.

**Adz** (Kenrick "Adz" Adlawan) is a Reader. He's one of the few people who can actually understand what BANTAY is saying and turn it into something normal people can use. He's smart, he's careful — too careful, the kind of guy who won't move until he's thought through every angle twice. That's about to be a problem, because he's going to notice something he wasn't supposed to notice. And once he sees it, the only safe thing left to do is the one thing he's spent his whole life avoiding.


---

## Adz (you, but made up)

Late 20s. Reads the machine the way other people read faces. Fast hands — he's the guy to beat at the reflex game on the common deck. He can swim, which barely anybody can anymore, and in a world this wet that's quietly a big deal. He wants more than a Reader's life. He wants to run something someday. He's also lazy in a specific way — he can't stand doing boring things by hand, so he builds little tools to do them for him and then takes the credit. His real problem: he plans instead of doing. He calls it being careful. It's mostly fear in a nicer outfit.
