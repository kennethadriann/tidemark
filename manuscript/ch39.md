## Chapter 39 — More Than One Fire

The down went on being the down for a long while after the far man's fire, and I went on giving it my legs, and there isn't much to tell about that stretch except that carrying a man's word in me had changed the walking. Before the fire I'd been reading the country to find out where it was taking me, watching the stone and the water for a hint of somebody's hand, guessing. Now I knew where it was taking me, more or less, and the country stopped being a thing I had to solve and turned back into plain distance, the kind you just spend. That's a different tiredness than the one I'd been carrying. Easier, even hungry, even with the cold still paying rent in my shirt. A man walks better toward a where than into a dark, and I hadn't known how much of my ache all this while had been the not-knowing until the not-knowing lifted a little and left only the walking behind.

The worse split came where he'd told me it would. I felt the way widen and the air change ahead of my feet, and then the passage opened its hand and there were two of it, the way there'd been two of it up at the fork a country ago. But it wasn't the same as the fork had been, and the difference wasn't in the stone. It was in me. Up at the first fork I'd had to stand in the cold sheet of water and read the two mouths and choose, nobody's mark to lean on, my own eye the only thing between the right dark and the wrong one. This time I had a man's mouth in my ear from an hour spent shutting my own. Low wet side, not the dry. So I didn't stand and read it. I didn't have to. The dry mouth climbed off to my left, drier and kinder-looking, the way a played-out thing will look kinder than the thing that's still worth working, and a month ago I might have believed my feet and taken it. Instead I gave it a glance the way you glance at a door you've already been told not to knock on, and I put my legs into the low one, into the wet, into the mean cold way that ran down and out toward the place with the men in it.

It was mean. He'd said low and wet and he'd been honest about both. The floor of it held standing water for long stretches, shin-deep and colder than the air, and I waded more than I walked, and the walls came in close enough that my shoulders learned them. I stopped keeping any count of it. I'd given up on counting anything I had no numbers for, and I had no numbers for this, only a day and a bit and a pair of legs he'd told me had to be honest, so I made them honest and kept them going and let the where pull me the way the down had been pushing me all this time.

I don't know when I first knew I was getting near. It didn't come as a thing you could point at. It came the way morning comes to a man with his eyes shut, a grayness before there's anything to see by it. The dark ahead stopped being the same all the way down. I noticed I'd started listening, which I hadn't been doing before because there'd been nothing to listen for but my own wet feet and the water working the stone, and a man quits hearing those. Now there was something under those. Not a voice. Not yet anything I could have named to you. Just a low made-ness in the air ahead, the far-off body of a sound the way you feel a big room in the dark before you touch a wall of it, and it wasn't the water and it wasn't me, and down here that left only the one thing it could be.

I made myself slow. That was the old work, the hardest work, the not-running-at-a-thing, and I'd got better at it than I'd ever wanted to. A man who comes fast out of the dark at a peopled place is a man who's answered a question nobody asked yet, and I'd walked too far to answer it wrong at the end. So I brought my pace down to a nothing and I let the near-ness come to me instead of chasing it, and it came. The sound firmed. It grew edges and then it grew a smell, and the smell was the thing that finally told me true, because it wasn't the deep's own cold breath that I'd been walking into since the fork, the low old water-smell of a country the sea had made. It was smoke. Not one fire's worth. Smoke with cooking under it and men under that, the thick lived-in reek of a place where people had been living and burning long enough to work it into the stone, the same reek the top stop had had before I'd ever laid an eye on the top stop, only smaller and deeper and further down the world than I'd have believed a smell like that could live.

And then the way bent, the last mean shin-deep bend of it, and gave me the sight to go with the smell.

It was below me and out ahead, down a long easing slope where the low wet way finally quit dropping and ran level toward a widening I couldn't see the far side of. Lights. Not a lamp, not the single fed still flame of the long run that burned for a man it would never meet. Fires. More than one of them. I stood at the top of the ease with the water still around my shins and I counted them without meaning to, the way I count everything, and there were more than one and more than two, low warm smudges of them scattered through the dark of the widening at no order I could read from up here, some near and some so far into the deep of the place they were only a redness on the underside of things. Between and around them the dark moved. People-shaped, load-shaped, the slow going-about of a place that had decided to be a place. The made sound I'd been walking toward for an hour was the sound of all of it at once, too far off still to break into anybody's particular voice, a town clearing its throat at the bottom of the world.

I didn't go down to it yet. I'd learned by now that the last of a thing is worth taking slow as the rest of it, and I was wet and empty and no-account looking, and a stranger walks into a peopled place better after he's stood a minute and let his own heart quit knocking. So I stood at the top of the ease where the fires couldn't see me and I looked at them, more than one of them, burning down there in the deep for men who were warming their hands at them right now the way I meant to warm mine, and after a while I found the slope with my feet and started down toward the light.

*End of Chapter 39.*
