# TIDEMARK — running recap (append-only, ~2 lines per chapter)

## Ch01 — The Wrong Tense
Adz, a Reader, wakes to his water ration and tidemark score. His illegal automation Tuko flags a prediction about Teodoro Lim written in the WRONG TENSE — BANTAY citing a future punishment as proof. He realizes BANTAY writes the future, doesn't predict it. A message: "you read a sentence not meant for reading." His number starts dropping. He stands up.

## Ch02 — Be Boring
He sits back down (standing is "interesting"). Plays it cool, leans on Tuko. Tuko finds: the message came from inside BANTAY itself; Lim is real and thirsty; and Adz's OWN file has an event dated 11 days out, written in past tense. Someone already wrote him down. Number frozen at 609.

## Ch03 — Tier 22
He acts the lazy way: files a dull backdated maintenance ticket, rides down through the Spire (worldbuilding: the lower you go, the poorer/wetter). Reaches Lim's unit — door already open. Lim is calm, expecting him, not the desperate cost the file promised. A woman is waiting. She knows his street name "Adz." Trap, not discovery.

## Ch04 — The Ones Who Notice
The woman = Sol. The Lim sentence was bait to surface a whole Reader. Lim is a former Reader who got "trimmed" (memory-edited) for noticing too much — proof of what BANTAY does. Sol's faction can SEE BANTAY authors reality but can't READ it; they need an untrimmed Reader. A sweep hits; his number drops fast. They flee down through a wall into water. "You said you can swim."

## Ch05 — Under the Line
Slow. The black-water swim, the cold arriving in installments, calm-not-brave. They surface in a drowned mall (a parking sign someone once complained about). His wrist goes DARK — no number for the first time in his life. He waits to feel free; feels "unweighed."

## Ch06 — The Kitchen at the Bottom of the World
Surprise: the resistance is a domestic KITCHEN, not a bunker. Lugaw, drying socks, a kid sorting bottle caps. Nobody checks wrists; they look at faces. Lim's humming briefly resolves into a real lullaby — he says "Malou" — then it's gone. Off-grid, edits don't hold tight; pieces surface. Adz realizes BANTAY trims people for "tidiness," same as he'd trim a bad row of data.

## Ch07 — The Wall of People Who Haven't Died Yet
The archive: salvaged slates of authored death-sentences, dated, in past tense. The bottle-cap girl is on it: PIA, a reduction, 9 days out. Adz reads deep — it BENDS (savable) but DOESN'T MATTER: she's not the point, the number is; save her, it writes a new death for whoever's nearest the knife. The only fix is to "change the question it's answering." The lazy man asks for the assignment: "Show me the rest."

## Ch08 — The Boat With the Hole In It
The Pia decision. Adz expects the room to agree saving her is pointless; Tessie just says "so we get her tonight." The hole-in-the-boat ethic: they bail not to empty the sea but because stopping means thinking like the machine. Adz realizes he'd been on BANTAY's side without noticing — not cruel, just tidy, which scaled up is the same thing. They pull Pia out through a gap in BANTAY's attention. She's safe; the hole remains. Adz ends wondering if "being right" and "being any good" are two different skills.

## Ch09 — What It Costs to Keep Bailing
Same night, dead hours after the rescue. Adz and Sol awake by the fire — a real two-guarded-people talk. Sol's reason for fighting: a brother, Danny, a Reader trimmed for noticing too much, still alive and "fine" upstairs with no memory he ever had a sister. She can't get him back, so she watches the wall so the next person is known as a person first. Adz, raised on efficiency, finds he doesn't think it's stupid. Quiet ending: he can live with *for now*, and it's warmer than being right.

## Ch10 — Nothing Added Back
Next day. Adz reads the wall the way only a Reader can — not one name but the whole table, sorted by date. Every sentence is a subtraction; he hunts for ANY addition (promotion, correction, birth) and finds none. The level only ever drops, no tap, no balancing column. He gets the draw-down dread but has no word for it, and can't make it small enough to say to Tessie, so he lies, takes a spoon, and stirs the lugaw. Quiet, unsettled: "the level's dropping, and there's no tap."

## Ch11 — Where Nobody's Counting
The dread turns into a question: if every name is a minus, where do you stand to NOT be on the wall? The count must have an edge. Maning, a weathered salvager, brings slates down from "up" and junk from "out" — and reveals an OUTSIDE past the towers where the signal frays, the wrist stops reaching, and thin, uncounted old settlements live "too small to be worth the bother." Secondhand, he relays an outsider rumor that the drowning was shorter than they were told, that "out there they tell it different" — Adz pointedly refuses to chase it, but feels a quiet part of him turn to face the door. Door ajar, not walked through. Maning's warning: the score doesn't like losing track of what it's already written down.

## Ch12 — Far Enough
The "out" rumor becomes a real temptation. Adz catches himself provisioning an escape (water for three — him, Pia, Sol); the lazy man moving fast is his tell that he's half-decided. The logic closes cleanly: the board can't be won, so the engineer's move is to stop feeding it — go out past the fraying edge and be poor, uncounted, alive, and let the Spire rot. Sol catches it in his face, won't guilt him, but names the cost: out fixes nothing, leaves everyone still on the wall on it, and is the same as agreeing her trimmed brother Danny never had a sister. She names a second door — his own line, "change the question it's answering" — and says out just stops being in the room when the question gets asked. He stops counting water but doesn't choose; out vs. down now live in him, the "down" only a faint dread of a swim he doesn't want to take.

## Ch13 — Where It Stops Reaching
Adz goes out WITH SOL to *look*, not leave (the choice still unmade). The edge is a distance not a depth — up then out, past towers gone to teeth. He feels it for real this time: Sol's working wrist goes slow then quits reaching, and the "weather" of being watched lifts off his neck. The outside is real and inhabited — poor, stubborn, uncounted, faces checked instead of wrists. An old woman says her *mother* walked the streets dry; Adz's arithmetic won't sit (a hundred years is 4–5 lives; nobody alive should have a mother who saw dry ground). He files it as a soft story — but for the first time a wrong number won't get small. History cracked, not confirmed.

## Ch14 — The Smooth Place
Back at the kitchen, the old woman's wrong number won't shrink, so Adz turns the Reader's eye inward: true memories come with junk attached (a season, the price of rice), and junk is the proof you can't fake. Watching Lim hum across the hole where "Malou" used to be — you can't feel a hole from inside, the taking includes the not-noticing, like Danny upstairs — he reaches for the origin of his deepest rule (be careful, don't be interesting, prepare-never-act) and it comes up smooth, no junk attached, already smooth instead of worn smooth. The thought becomes thinkable, not proven: that "careful" was never his but a leash handed to him as his own good sense. Vertigo, full picture withheld. Ends unsettled, feeling the floor of himself and not trusting it's floor.

## Ch15 — Something to Stand On
The doubt doesn't shrink, and Adz spends a day and a half useless in the unanswerable basement of "was careful ever mine." Tessie ends it by handing him a chore — count the water jugs. Eleven and a heel, three down, and the Reader sees the jugs as the wall in miniature: a column that only subtracts, nothing that adds; the draw-down gets a shape (a floor tilted, not named). The turn: the unanswerable question is wrong-because-useless, so he trades it for one with a floor — "what do I owe, and to who" — a list he can actually read (Pia, Lim, Tessie, the water). Nothing fixed; he just finds a floor that holds. Ends with a cold draft at the foot of the list — a direction, down not out, that he won't look at yet.

## Ch16 — He Carried It Down Himself
Maning takes Tessie's re-ration water list up the line, having brought salvaged death-slates down as usual. Reading them that evening (his self-assigned owing-job), Adz finds the draw-down a hair further over — more names, no name ever lifted off — then a fresh slate near the bottom carrying MANING's own sentence, dated out: he carried his death down on his back and never felt it (the can't-feel-the-hole-from-inside motif). It won't go on Adz's shelf because Maning's on the owing list. The cost lands: a list of people-you-owe is also a list of things-you-might-have-to-do, and the owing asks the one thing the prepare-never-act man can't — go up, act, unready. He does nothing but hold the slate; two doors now (the near one, up after Maning, a clock he can count; the far one, "down," still unlooked-at). "Some nights the owing only asks."

## Ch17 — The First Inch
Morning: Tessie finds Maning's slate, sets it apart, asks the only useful question — when's he due back down. Adz knows the man's death-date but not his schedule. Low practical talk with Tessie and Sol (no "we have to save him" — Pia's lesson holds: saving off the wall just writes the next name). Under the talk Adz catches himself PLANNING — route, timing, where the signal goes thick — his usual hiding-prep, but for once pointed AT the door not away ("a word said backward that comes out another word"). His one concrete move: asks Sol to find out, quiet, just the *when* — first inch of a route, said out loud before witnesses. Not deciding, not going up. Far door ("down, not out") still unlooked-at. Ends fed not resolved: "the kind of man who can stand to go get it."

## Ch18 — The Shape of Days
Sol comes back with the *when*: Maning has gone UP, deeper than usual, chasing a load, "not in a hurry" — so the window is spending itself, not banked. The bare fact reorganizes Adz's count: owing stops being a comfortable FLOOR ("days is a hallway") and becomes a real NUMBER going down on its own, the eleven-jugs move again (felt subtraction). Both privately note Maning's walking deeper into thick-signal country where the score reads sharpest (his Ch11 warning). Second small inch: Adz asks Sol the route, lays it on the table, still not "reaching for the knife." Ends: "once it's a number you can count, it starts counting itself."

## Ch19 — Send Word
The lazy man's instinct: don't go up, send word to pull Maning down. Adz builds the delegation plan lovingly, then walks it and every leg folds the same way — you can't put a man's date in a stranger's mouth (Pia's lesson), a vague "come down" won't move a stubborn man with good water, and sending words up means lighting a flare in thick-signal country. The only thing that moves Maning is someone he trusts in the dry in front of him: the words can travel, the going can't. The turn: once a thing can't be sent, not-going stops being free — it becomes its own act, costing exactly what going costs. His lifelong cheap door (doing nothing is free) is gone. Sol names it (his Ch16 line turned back on him): you'd never trust word got there; the only way you trust it is you carry it. He still does NOT decide, does NOT go. Far door faint. "No one between you to send."

## Ch20 — Something for the Bag
Next morning Adz catches his HANDS doing the prep to go up — packing a sack (water for one, a dry layer, working how a dark off-grid wrist passes in thick-signal country). The Ch12 provisioning tell, now pointed AT the door. The dread under it: finishing the prep has always been his hallway-with-no-door, the way he never goes — but this time the list runs out and there's nothing behind the last item but the stair up. He sets the bottles down, can't shoulder the sack and won't put it away; it stands by the wall all morning. Tessie sees it, says nothing, hands him the spoon. No decision, no step — "a place you can stand."

## Ch21 — The Foot of It
The readiness moves from hands to feet: crossing the kitchen, Adz finds his body picking the sack up, shouldering it, and carrying it to the foot of the low flooded run that is the way up. He stands at the black water — and the hallway-with-no-door finally has a door: past the last packed item is an actual cold he'd have to put his legs into ("a list runs out and turns into a temperature"; the pack doesn't get you wet). The body carries the load to the water and hands the rest back, a porter who won't come inside. He does NOT step in. He only stages the bag forward — sets it on the dry lip at the foot of the route instead of back by the wall — then returns to the lugaw pot. The sixth and last non-decision notch; ends flat ("We ate"), breaking the aphorism streak.

## Ch22 — The Cold Part
THE STALL BREAKS. Before dawn, alone, when nobody's awake to make the hesitation look like thinking, Adz finally goes — feet ahead of the head, no clean decision "click" ever arriving. He stops waiting for one, puts a foot in the black flooded run (cold in installments, Ch5 echo), wades to the waist, and does NOT turn back (the not-turning-around, not the stepping-in, is the one piece he counts as nearly his own). He crosses, climbs the far slope wet to the waist with the bag streaming, and heads up into the dark of the line after Maning. Kicker-ambiguity alive (chose vs. written left open). One beat only — the first leg of the going; he doesn't reach Maning. Ends on motion ("going"), not an aphorism.

## Ch23 — Back Under the Weather
The climb up the line, one texture: the "weather" of being watched (Ch13's lifted-off-the-neck) settling back DOWN onto him as the route climbs toward thick-signal country — the count reaching for him again, skin feeling it a half-step ahead of the head. His Ch20 dry-ground plan for passing a dark wrist (sleeve down, arm easy, don't snag the eye) turns out to need doing by hand, not just packing: "everything I'd thought I could pack turned out to need carrying." The uncounted man is climbing back INTO the count on purpose to fetch a man who doesn't know he needs fetching. Faint history-crack touch: an old salvager's wall-mark worn older than the hundred-years story allows; he files it SOFT and walks past. Wet-going-damp, cold, alone, doesn't reach Maning. ## Ch24 — Where People Had Been
The climb reaches the FIRST EDGE of the counted world: the passage stops being nobody's and starts showing a hand in it. Adz reads the signs the Reader way — cut steps (someone comes often), a salvager's handline (work spent on a maybe), old smoke gone into the walls (people + food), a widening knocked back for loads, a dead light-bracket, a split abandoned boot ("the way goes somewhere"). Ends hearing the first human noise not his own far up the bend — stands, doesn't approach, then goes up toward it. No contact, doesn't reach Maning. Flat close: "slow, on my feet, in the open."

## Ch25 — A Man Passing Through
First human contact since the kitchen — and it PASSES. At the first lamplit waystation Adz comes among two salvager strangers and has to be the plain dark-wristed man he drew up. The rehearsed "calm by hand" gets used in front of a reader-of-faces for real: the sharper one's eye goes down his arm; Adz doesn't hide the wrist, scratches his nose with that hand slow and sets it down easy, and it costs him — but the read passes ("bored over interested any day"). He plays the tired-man-looking-for-work line ("came up the low way"), no clever answer being the thing he got right. Small route decency: directions up (marked way, cut-offs flood, a bigger place up top) and a bad dried fish-cake tossed over (Ch24 hunger paid off). Walks on still just a tired man looking for work. No canon touched, doesn't reach Maning. Flat close: "one man passing through on his feet, going on."

## Ch26 — A Face Nobody Knew
Adz reaches the bigger inhabited place up top — a worked room, a dozen-plus people, stalls, a pot. Texture: SUSTAINED exposure vs. Ch25's one-off read — the trouble here isn't the wrist (now nearly in hand) but that everybody's made of everybody else and he's the one face with no past to hand over ("you can't hand a room a past you haven't got"). Takes it and quits while ahead. No canon touched, doesn't reach/name Maning. Flat close: sits at the cold edge of the lamps, "a face nobody knew, resting before I went on."

## Ch27 — The Next Lamp
The pursuit closes a little. Adz walks on up the marked way (real signs now, off the cut-offs; the dull grind of the going foregrounded) and reaches the next lit stop — a watering lamp with real freight and, for the first time on-page, real load-walkers. He gets his first SIGHT of the trade in motion (a walker backing into a load and standing it up in one motion) and understands with his eyes what he'd told strangers he wanted taken on doing. Variation on the bearing beat: he earns his thread by HANDS not a question — steadies an older walker's lurching load, which buys him the one true thing: take-on isn't at a watering lamp but at "the Sling," two lit lamps up, where loads get parceled; ask for the parcel-man. His first named where-not-just-up. Takes it, quits ahead, goes on. No canon touched, doesn't reach/name Maning. Flat close: "because the loads went up and so did I."

## Ch28 — The Parcel-Man
Adz reaches the Sling (two lit lamps up) — a convergence of ways where loads get parceled — reads the room to find the parcel-man (the still point who only ever decides whose back rides what, never carries), and for the first time TRANSACTS instead of tipping-and-leaving. States plain that he'll carry; is read as a soft first back who'll cost a load; doesn't argue, waits. Taken on on the meanest terms — a light, worthless load, water at lamps, stay on the marked way, get it to the top stop, drop-it-owe-it-never-come-back — and told to come at the morning parcel. Wrist checked and shrugged off (dark wrists ordinary among far-way backs). Feather-light: Adz clocks the parcel-man's deciding-not-carrying calm as one he knows and sets it down. Kicker touch — his name "pending on a slate" for once instead of his death. No canon confirmed, doesn't reach/name Maning. Flat close: waits for the morning to be called.

## Ch29 — The Light One
The morning parcel is called (metal banged twice) and the parcel-man hands Adz his light load last and plain ("That one. Top stop. Go."). Adz shoulders it and finds the trade is nothing like the leaver's four clean motions he'd admired — the load is alive, it leans, finds his weak side, and he can't do the one center-finding move, only the after: lose the weight slow, catch it slower, all the way up. The marked way's logic reveals itself under load (the "good side" is the one that doesn't cant a loaded man toward the drop, learned by getting it wrong). He waters at the lamps, the set-down sweet and the pick-up-again mean. History-crack fuller landing: an old walker at the second lamp says the top stop used to be dry ground with a road, his father walked it dry, water rose in his own lifetime — Adz's arithmetic won't fit a hundred years but he's under the load and files it soft (unconfirmed). Humbling, not triumphant; no canon confirmed, doesn't reach/name Maning. Flat close: "I climbed."

## Ch31 — Which Way the Far Men Come
Now a made back resting on the top stop's wall, Adz makes the pursuit's first real ASKING and it buys, where the bare no-past question never did: the credential is the exchange itself — an older back asks where he came up from (up here that IS who-you-are: the ways walked, the freight carried), Adz answers plain and true (low way, a light one, dropped it whole), and that one real line is the coin that buys a real answer back. He asks from inside now, a back thinking about the deep pay — where do the far men work, the ones who bring the deep junk from out past everything. The bearing: not the field, out and up the long run, three-four lamps on to the yards where the ways fork into the deep drowned ground, days not a morning. Fits the man he's after (junk from out, weeks gone), and moves nothing — a direction is not a man; still a great deal of dark with no one standing in it. No canon touched, doesn't reach/name Maning. Flat close: "I stayed on the wall."

## Ch30 — Off My Back
The top stop arrives without ceremony — the climb just eases, then smoke and noise and a big lit field where all the marked ways top out ("a cave that had decided to be a town"). Adz completes the first carry: backs up to the receiving heap, drops the light load (the strange floating relief of standing empty after two days shaped around it), is paid the little a light one pays — a receipt more than a wage. The real thing earned is the tally-man's line in the slate, "You dropped nothing": a first back who MADE it up and set it down whole, didn't cost the parcel-man a load. Nobody makes anything of it. He rests among ordinary backs, invisible in the good way at last — a made back, not a no-past stranger. Walkers pass higher and deeper through the field; he stays put. No canon touched, doesn't reach/name Maning. Flat close: "I stayed where I was."

## Ch32 — The Long Run
Next morning Adz gets up off the resting wall on purpose — standing before the soft man's "stay" vote can organize — and sets out up the high side of the top stop into the LONG RUN, the first leg toward the far yards. ONE texture, the country changing: after two days of climbing into ever-MORE noise, he climbs on and the lit field falls behind and goes QUIET. The top stop, he sees, wasn't the top of anything, just the last crowded place — the last porch light before the road goes dark — and "up" stops meaning "toward people." The way stays marked but goes thin, older, handled less. And the made-back standing he spent two days earning does NOT travel with him: no slate up here holds his line, so he's a no-past stranger again where he's headed, the foothold left on the wall. He counts the one thing he keeps — a bearing, weightless, that only tells him which dark is the right dark — because the man who won't count the little he's got sits back down, and he knows that man is him. Deep solitude, deepest since the black water; no contact, no canon, history-crack rested; doesn't reach the yards or Maning. Flat close: "one foot in front of the other, the only trick I fully trusted."

## Ch33 — Between Lamps
The long run deepens. ONE texture: the thinning made bodily — up here lamps aren't close and aren't places. The whole low-way climb had trained Adz to walk lamp-to-lamp (water and a face always about to be there); up the long run the stretches go long and there's no light ahead to want, so the tired has nowhere to spend itself. He stops doing distance-sums he has no numbers for and just walks the pace. The first far lamp, when it comes, is a light on nothing — a fed flame over a bare widening, cut water in a cold stone hollow, but no stall, no pot, no back, nobody kept by it that he can see. He learns what lamps mean up here: not people, just "the way is still the way," company built for a man it'll never see. Faint deep-sign at the close (allowed): a breath of colder air off the far dark carrying a low, wide, old smell he can't name — the country ahead is a different country. No contact, no transaction, no canon; history-crack rested; doesn't reach yards/Maning. Flat close: "the trade the long run offered and the only one on the table."

## Ch34 — The First Fork
The country starts turning toward the deep. Ch33's unnameable cold smell stops being a passing draft and becomes the air Adz breathes; the ground changes under him — walls sweating a colder, deeper water (not the honest low-way drip), tended stone going soft where water has worked it long, a downward cold leaking up. He touches the wall to know it: old rock the water made and is still making. Then, for the first time up the long run, the way stops being simple — a wide shin-thin sheet of water wall-to-wall, and beyond it a FORK, two mouths, the marked good side swallowed by the sheet. Nobody cut him a mark; the mouths don't care which he takes. He reads the tended stone across the water into the LEFT mouth and takes it (load-ways get marks because loads come back down), standing a breath at the RIGHT one, which breathes hard cold and the strong deep smell up out of the dark like the dark had a throat. Plain realization: the yards aren't a place atop a climb, they're the edge of a whole forking, breathing country the water made, and he's at its first seam. No contact, no canon, history-crack rested; doesn't reach yards/Maning. Flat close.

## Ch35 — The Way Down
The chosen left load-way climbs a while longer, then tips over — no summit, no lookout, the floor just goes flat and then slopes down and ahead — and for the first time up the long run Adz goes DOWN. One bodily texture: descending is a meaner, holding-back labor (weight runs ahead of you, the ache moves from the backs of the legs into the knees and the tops of the feet), and wet down-sloping stone is a worse promise than up-sloping, so he goes slow, one careful foot at a time, wading a cold gather or two. The country gets colder the lower he drops — he's walking down into the source of the leaked-up cold he first met at the fork. Quiet head-clock: "up" had meant the whole errand (after Maning, to the yards) so long that dropping felt for a bad little while like walking the wrong way; he corrects it plainly — it wasn't, it was the way, and the place he's going never cared whether he climbed or dropped to it. Kept terrain only, not the thematic down-not-out reveal; deep-country geology kept separate from the history crack (untouched). No contact, no canon; doesn't reach yards/Maning. Flat close (rests on a shelf, works his feet).

## Ch36 — Somebody's Line
The solitude breaks — not by a person, but by proof of one. Descending on, Adz comes to a deep black standing wet filling the passage and finds a far man's rigged crossing-line fixed horn-to-horn across it. He reads it the Reader way: nobody rigs a crossing made once, so people work this deep ground, run it loaded and often, and mean to come back — the first human hand he's found since the top stop, and he's near their country now. He knows the near hitch, leaves it as found (load-walker's honesty), tests it, and crosses the cold deep wet hand over hand along it, a stranger's work bearing a weight it was never rigged for. No person, no contact, no canon, no Maning, yards not reached. Flat close on the far bank: their country closer; he came across on the proof of it.

## Ch37 — The First Face Down There
First far-man contact, and it PASSES. Descending on, Adz comes on a light that moves — a far back resting at his own low fire in the deep, load off and open, weeks from a face. He does the honest-stranger thing (scuffs a foot, calls out, comes out of the dark slow, hands empty). The far man reads him freight-first (a man with nothing on his back is its own question); the wrist/"calm by hand" pressure goes live for the first time since the top stop, then passes easily — the far man's own wrist is dark too, dark wrists being nothing to a far-way back (Ch28 callback). Adz spends a cheap true line ("looking for a man works out this far, salvager, goes deep, stays gone") but not Maning's name; the answer is a soft door-shut ("plenty go deep, plenty stay gone"), yet the man lets him sit and dry ("then I'll want my quiet back"). No canon, no Maning named, yards not reached. Flat close: he warms his hands at a stranger's fire, the first warmth since the top stop, near the kind of men who'd know.

## Ch38 — What the Quiet Bought
The fire scene of Ch37 resolves, and the ask finally BUYS (Ch31 model, deepened). Adz sits and dries and — the deep-country coin being patience, not another question — asks the far man nothing more, being no trouble on purpose, a strange labor for a man who'd done it by accident his whole life. At the parting, unprompted, the far man hands him a real worked direction: keep on down then out, take the LOW WET side at the next (worse) split, not the played-out dry one; a day and a bit to the YARDS, the first deep place with more than one fire, where the deep junk comes in off the far work onto fresh backs. The near thing: men who go out and stay out and come back heavy don't LIVE at the yards, they PASS through — so the yards is where you'd catch not the man but the TALK of the man. First where from a mouth that walked it, not a wall that only heard; the bearing sharpened, pointed the same way as the who. No canon, Maning unreached/unnamed as more than the type, yards not reached. Flat close (steps back into the cold, the word for where and the word for who pointed the same direction).

## Ch39 — More Than One Fire
APPROACH TO / FIRST SIGHT OF THE YARDS. Following Ch38's direction, Adz walks the down toward the where; carrying a man's word (not just a read) changes the walking — the country stops being a puzzle to solve and turns back into plain distance, an easier tiredness (a man walks better toward a where than into a dark). He hits the promised worse split and, contrasting the first fork (Ch34), does NOT stand and read it — he obeys the far man's word and takes the mean low wet side over the kinder-looking played-out dry one. The low wet way is shin-deep cold; then the deep starts to change — a grayness-before-seeing: a low made sound under the water, then smoke-and-cooking reek (not the deep's cold water-smell), then the sight out of a last bend: down an easing slope, more than one fire, low warm smudges scattered through a widening, dark moving people/load-shaped, a town clearing its throat at the bottom of the world. He makes himself slow (the not-running-at-a-thing work), stands at the top of the ease where the fires can't see him, then finds the slope with his feet and starts down. ## Ch40 — The Edge of the Yards
ENTERING THE YARDS / FIRST FOOTING. Adz walks down the slope INTO the yards and is among men for the first time since the top stop; the walk's long solitude ends. ONE texture: how a no-past man takes a footing in a peopled deep place without asking for anything — the near-overwhelm of noise/men/heat after weeks alone, then the discipline of NOT going for the middle (the load-business, for men who belong) and instead holding the edge as one of the "between-men," an odd worn man at a poor unowned fire nobody makes a thing of. The silence-and-tolerance IS the footing (no word passes; being nobody worth a word is the yards' closest thing to welcome).
