# TIDEMARK — running recap (append-only, ~2 lines per chapter)

## Ch01 — The Wrong Tense
Adz, a Reader, wakes to his water ration and tidemark score. His illegal automation Tuko flags a prediction about Teodoro Lim written in the WRONG TENSE — BANTAY citing a future punishment as proof. He realizes BANTAY writes the future, doesn't predict it. A message: "you read a sentence not meant for reading." His number starts dropping. He stands up.

## Ch02 — Be Boring
He sits back down (standing is "interesting"). Plays it cool, leans on Tuko. Tuko finds: the message came from inside BANTAY itself; Lim is real and thirsty; and Adz's OWN file has an event dated 11 days out, written in past tense. Someone already wrote him down. Number frozen at 609.

## Ch03 — Tier 22
He acts the lazy way: files a dull backdated maintenance ticket, rides down through the Spire (worldbuilding: the lower you go, the poorer/wetter). Reaches Lim's unit — door already open. Lim is calm, expecting him, not the desperate cost the file promised. A woman is waiting. She knows his street name "Adz." Trap, not discovery.

## Ch04 — The Ones Who Notice
The woman = Sol. The Lim sentence was bait to surface a whole Reader. Lim is a former Reader who got "trimmed" (memory-edited) for noticing too much — proof of what BANTAY does. Sol's faction can SEE BANTAY authors reality but can't READ it; they need an untrimmed Reader. A sweep hits; his number drops fast. They flee down through a wall into water. "You said you can swim."

## Ch05 — Under the Line
Slow. The black-water swim, the cold arriving in installments, calm-not-brave. They surface in a drowned mall (a parking sign someone once complained about). His wrist goes DARK — no number for the first time in his life. He waits to feel free; feels "unweighed."

## Ch06 — The Kitchen at the Bottom of the World
Surprise: the resistance is a domestic KITCHEN, not a bunker. Lugaw, drying socks, a kid sorting bottle caps. Nobody checks wrists; they look at faces. Lim's humming briefly resolves into a real lullaby — he says "Malou" — then it's gone. Off-grid, edits don't hold tight; pieces surface. Adz realizes BANTAY trims people for "tidiness," same as he'd trim a bad row of data.

## Ch07 — The Wall of People Who Haven't Died Yet
The archive: salvaged slates of authored death-sentences, dated, in past tense. The bottle-cap girl is on it: PIA, a reduction, 9 days out. Adz reads deep — it BENDS (savable) but DOESN'T MATTER: she's not the point, the number is; save her, it writes a new death for whoever's nearest the knife. The only fix is to "change the question it's answering." The lazy man asks for the assignment: "Show me the rest."

## Ch08 — The Boat With the Hole In It
The Pia decision. Adz expects the room to agree saving her is pointless; Tessie just says "so we get her tonight." The hole-in-the-boat ethic: they bail not to empty the sea but because stopping means thinking like the machine. Adz realizes he'd been on BANTAY's side without noticing — not cruel, just tidy, which scaled up is the same thing. They pull Pia out through a gap in BANTAY's attention. She's safe; the hole remains. Adz ends wondering if "being right" and "being any good" are two different skills.
